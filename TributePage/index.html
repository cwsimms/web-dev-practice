Steve Wozniak

The genius behind the genius

Steve Wozniak demonstrating the Apple IIgs in 1986.

  • 1950: Born in San Jose, California.
  • ~1972: Designed "blue boxes" for making free long-distance phone calls.
  • 1975: Began work on the Apple 1 Computer.
  • 1976: Cofounded Apple Computer Inc. with Steve Jobs and Ronald Wayne. Completed work on the Apple 1, which would go on to sell an initial 50 units at $666.66 per unit. Mike Markkula provides venture capital to the fledgling company in November of that year.
  • 1977: Designs the Apple II, the first personal computer to display color graphics. The Apple II would go on to become a tremendous success, even outselling the later Macintosh until the late 1980s.
  • 1981: Wozniak is involved in a plane crash and sustained serious injuries, which included amnesia for five weeks. Afterwards, Wozniak temporarily steps away from active involvement with Apple and re-enrolls at UC Berkeley.
  • 1982-1983: Founds Unuson and sponsors two US Festivals, which combined technology and rock music. After losing millions on both, Wozniak returns to Apple Computer in late 1983 as an engineer.
  • 1985: After the Apple II team is snubbed repeatedly by the Macintosh team (despite superior sales), Wozniak grows disillusioned with Apple's direction and quits.
  • 1987: After founding CL 9 in 1985, Wozniak developed and sold the "CORE", the first universal programmable remote control.
  • 2001: Founds Wheels of Zeus to develop GPS-related products.
  • 2002: Joins the board of directors for Ripcord Netwokrs, Inc. and Danger Inc.
  • 2006: Shuts down Wheels of Zeus and founds Acquicor Technology.
  • 2009-2014: Chief Scientist at Fusion-io.
  • 2014: Chief Scientist at Primary Data.
  • 2015: Cofounds Silicon Valley Comic Con with Rick White. Trip Hunter is made CEO.
  • 2017: Founds Woz U.
  • 2018: Woz U is licensed with the State of Arizona.
  • 2020: Launches Efforce, an online marketplace for the funding of ecologically friendly projects. Also created the WOZX cryptocurrency, which raised 1,400% in its first week of trading.
  • 2021: Cofounds Privateer Space with Alex Fielding.
